System Architecture

Flocking Core &
Skeletal Synapse

HYAKUCHŌ is driven by the seamless convergence of sub-millimeter computer vision tracking and edge-computed biological flocking rules. No centralized server, no manual remote control.

01 / INTERFACE

Gesture Synapse

Using real-time posture mapping operating at 120Hz, the system captures skeletal joint angles of the wearer's arm. This coordinates relative coordinate grids dynamically, transforming simple arm extensions, open palms, and sweeps into immediate swarm vector forces.

Status: Synapse Active (120 FPS)
02 / DECENTRALIZED COGNITION

Swarm Commander

Instead of single-drone piloting, the entire swarm behaves as a unified biological entity. Leveraging edge calculations of Craig Reynolds' Boids algorithms (Cohesion, Alignment, and Separation), the butterflies maintain spatial coherence while avoiding local obstacles.

Core: Emergent Vector Fields
03 / POSITIONING

Localization & Comm

Four micro-beacons integrated into the launcher backpack emit high-frequency telemetry pulses. The swarm intercepts these pulses, calculating sub-millimeter relative positioning with an outstanding latency of under 8.5ms.

Latency: ~ 8.5ms (Sub-millimeter)
04 / LIFECYCLE

Inductive Recovery

When the recall signal is triggered, electromagnetic nodes in the launchpad bays activate. The butterflies navigate individually, locking onto magnets to establish wireless inductive charging connections and prepare for the next release loop.

Charge System: Inductive Loop
